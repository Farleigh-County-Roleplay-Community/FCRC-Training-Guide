Trainees

    Farleigh County Training Guide

    Welcome trainers! Use this guide to run your sessions step by step.

    Intro

    Hello! My name is [INSERT NAME], and I’ll be your cohost today. Your training will be split into two categories: Theoretical and Physical. Within each are different activities you will complete step by step.

    Instructor: Greet trainees, introduce yourself, and explain the training structure clearly before starting.

    ###### Now we’re done with this section. Let’s go to the next section!

    Starter Quiz (Theoretical)

    Please answer the following questions one by one:

    1. What does FRP mean in ER:LC, and why is it important to avoid?
    2. Explain what “GTA Driving” means and why it is not allowed.
    3. What is the maximum speed limit in civilian areas unless otherwise stated?
    4. What should you do if you encounter a priority situation while on patrol?

    Instructor: Ask questions one by one. Give trainees time to respond. Ensure understanding before moving forward.

    ###### Now we’re done with this section. Let’s go to the next section!

    Scenario Quiz (Theoretical)

    You will be given scenarios to explain how you would respond:

    1. You see someone running a red light in River City. What do you do?
    2. A civilian reports reckless driving. How do you proceed?
    3. You encounter a roleplayer refusing to comply. How do you handle it?

    Instructor: Present each scenario individually. Evaluate reasoning, not just the answer. Encourage professionalism.

    ###### Now we’re done with this section. Let’s go to the next section!

    Driving Test (Physical)

    Drive from the Sheriff’s Office to River City Bank, following all traffic laws.

    Instructor: Observe for safe turns, speed limits, signaling, and roleplay standards.

    ###### Now we’re done with this section. Let’s go to the next section!

    Patrolling (Physical)

    You will patrol River City and monitor for rule breakers.

    Instructor: Trainers will act as rule breakers. Evaluate how trainees respond, ensuring they stay professional.

    ###### Now we’re done with this section. Let’s go to the next section!

    Driving Test 2 (Physical)

    Drive from River City to Springfield Main Street, following all traffic laws.

    Instructor: Same as first driving test, but with more traffic interactions.

    ###### Now we’re done with this section. Let’s go to the next section!

    Scenarios (Physical)

    Each trainee will complete 2 moderator calls. Examples:

    • Reckless driver in River City
    • Civilian dispute in Springfield
    • Traffic collision on highway
    • Bank robbery roleplay

    Instructor: Provide realistic mod calls. Observe decision-making, teamwork, and communication.

    ###### Now we’re done with this section. Let’s go to the next section!

    Command Practice (Physical)

    Demonstrate knowledge of common commands:

    • How do you set a priority timer?
    • How do you check active moderators?
    • How do you issue a server message?
    • Final command: /load to return to the meeting room.

    Instructor: Ensure trainees know each command and when to use it.

    ###### Now we’re done with this section. Let’s go to the next section!

    Ending Questions (Theoretical)

    Final questions to conclude training:

    • What’s the most important rule in ER:LC roleplay?
    • How do you handle a disrespectful civilian?
    • Why is teamwork important in patrols?

    Instructor: Discuss answers and give feedback before proceeding to results.

    ###### Now we’re done with this section. Let’s go to the next section!

    Results

    Trainees will stand in formation. If passed: “Congratulations, you’ve passed training!” If failed: “Unfortunately, you did not pass. Please reapply after further preparation.”

    Instructor: Deliver results clearly, fairly, and respectfully.
